Why Burmese Jadeite Is the Benchmark

The origin that defines quality, rarity and long-term value.

Not all jadeite comes from the same place

Jadeite can be found in different parts of the world, including Guatemala, Russia and Japan.

However, when it comes to fine gem-quality jadeite, one origin consistently stands apart: Myanmar (Burma).

Burmese jadeite is not just popular — it is the global reference point.

Geological conditions matter

The formation of jadeite requires extremely specific geological conditions — high pressure, specific mineral composition and long formation periods.

Myanmar’s jadeite deposits have produced stones with:

• finer structure • higher translucency • richer colour distribution

These qualities are rarely matched elsewhere at the same level.

Why collectors prioritise Burmese jadeite

Collectors are not simply buying colour. They are looking for material that holds:

• structural integrity • long-term stability • rarity • historical significance

This is why Burmese Type A jadeite remains the standard.

Historical and cultural importance

For centuries, Burmese jadeite has been traded, treasured and worn across Asia.

It is deeply connected to heritage, craftsmanship and generational wealth.

This historical continuity strengthens its position in the market today.

Why not Guatemala or other sources?

Other sources can still produce jadeite.

However, they often differ in:

• structure (less refined) • translucency (lower) • colour distribution (less balanced)

This does not mean they are without value — but they are not considered the same benchmark.

The difference is not obvious at first glance

To an untrained eye, jadeite from different origins may appear similar.

But under proper observation, differences in depth, light interaction and structure become clear.

This is why experienced buyers prefer to view jadeite in person before making decisions.
