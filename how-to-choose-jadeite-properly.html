How to Choose Jadeite Properly

Not by colour alone, and not by first impressions alone — but by what the stone truly reveals.

Practical Understanding

Choosing jadeite properly begins with knowing what to look for.

A beautiful piece may catch the eye quickly. A strong piece holds attention for deeper reasons.

Begin with whether it is natural.

Before colour, size or style, the first question should always be whether the jadeite is natural Type A.

Without that foundation, everything else becomes less meaningful. Appearance can be created. Integrity cannot.

The first step in choosing jadeite properly is knowing what the stone truly is.

Look at structure, not only colour.

Structure refers to the grain and internal formation of the jadeite. Fine structure often appears smoother, denser and more refined.

Coarser structure may still have colour, but it often lacks the same calmness and depth when seen closely.

Observe translucency carefully.

Translucency is how light moves through the stone. It is one of the most important elements in fine jadeite.

A more translucent piece often feels softer, deeper and more alive. This quality is especially important when judging finer material.

Jadeite does not rely on sparkle. It relies on how light rests within it.

Notice texture under light.

Texture is the visual feel of the material. A refined texture appears even, smooth and gentle. A rougher texture may look drier or less cohesive.

Texture helps reveal how fine the stone truly is beyond the first layer of colour.

Judge colour for quality, not only strength.

Strong colour can be attractive, but quality of colour matters more than loudness of colour.

Ask whether the tone is balanced, natural, even, and whether it sits well within the structure of the jadeite.

Softer colour in stronger material can often feel more valuable than brighter colour in weaker material.

Consider proportion and craftsmanship.

A well-made piece feels balanced. The proportions should support the material, and the finishing should allow the jadeite to speak rather than fight against it.

Good craftsmanship rarely feels excessive. It feels resolved.

Do not judge from one angle only.

Jadeite should be turned, lifted and seen under more than one light source if possible.

A piece may behave differently in soft light, stronger light or natural daylight. This is why first impressions should never be the only guide.

A single photograph or angle can never fully represent jadeite.

Compare pieces before deciding.

Proper comparison sharpens judgment. It helps reveal what one piece has that another lacks — whether in texture, translucency, colour balance or overall presence.

Often, the right piece becomes clearer only after comparison.

Pay attention to presence.

Some jadeite pieces feel calm, balanced and complete. Others may be attractive at first glance but do not hold the same weight when observed longer.

This is what many experienced buyers recognise. Not just beauty, but presence.

At Ixchell, understanding comes before pressure.

We believe jadeite should be chosen properly, not hurriedly. That means observing, comparing, asking, and allowing a piece to reveal itself.

The goal is not to push a decision. It is to help the right piece become clear.

Jadeite is not chosen well by looking faster.
It is chosen well by looking properly.
