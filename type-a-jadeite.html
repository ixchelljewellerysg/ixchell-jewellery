What is Type A Jadeite?

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated, dyed, or polymer impregnated. It retains its original mineral structure, colour, and integrity.

This is the most important concept to understand before choosing jadeite. Without this knowledge, it is easy to misunderstand value, quality, and authenticity.

Learn first. Then decide.

Why is Type A jadeite important?

Jadeite is not judged by beauty alone. Treatment affects structure, durability, and long-term value. Type A jadeite remains stable over time and is traditionally preferred for fine jewellery and collection.

Treated jadeite may look attractive at first, but its condition and value may change over time.

Is Type A jadeite the same as “real jade”?

The term “real jade” is often used loosely. Jade may refer to jadeite or nephrite, and may also include treated stones.

A better question is: what type of jade is it, and has it been treated?

Clarity removes confusion.

Does Type A jadeite guarantee quality?

No. Type A only refers to treatment. Quality still depends on colour, translucency, texture, structure, proportion, and craftsmanship.

Two Type A jadeite pieces can look completely different in value and appearance.

Why should jadeite be viewed in person?

Jadeite changes under different lighting, angles, and skin tones. Photographs cannot fully show its depth, texture, and presence.

This is why proper viewing matters.

What you see in person is what you are choosing.
