What Is Type A Jadeite?

A clear explanation of natural jadeite, why Type A matters, and why serious buyers prefer to understand jadeite properly before choosing it.

Type A jadeite means natural jadeite.

In simple terms, Type A jadeite refers to jadeite that has not been chemically treated to improve its appearance.

It may be cut, polished and shaped, but it has not been bleached, polymer-filled or artificially altered in the way lower-trust material often is.

Type A jadeite is valued not only for how it looks, but for what it is.

Why does this matter?

Many people first notice jadeite by colour. Yet colour alone does not explain quality, value or confidence in purchase.

Natural Type A jadeite matters because it preserves the integrity of the stone. For buyers, collectors and those choosing a meaningful piece, that integrity is central.

Natural character Type A jadeite keeps the structure and life of the stone intact.
Confidence in selection Buyers know they are choosing natural jadeite rather than material improved by treatment.
Long-term appreciation Understanding what the piece truly is helps buyers make more informed decisions.
Meaning beyond appearance For many, jadeite is not just decorative. Authenticity matters.

What is the difference between Type A and treated jadeite?

Type A jadeite

Natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated to alter its structure.

Treated jadeite

Some material on the market may be bleached, filled or otherwise enhanced to improve appearance. This may make it look cleaner or more attractive at first glance, but it is not the same as natural Type A jadeite.

The difference is not merely visual. It is structural, material and trust-based.

Can you tell just from a photo?

Not properly.

Photos can suggest colour, shape and surface. They rarely show the full character of jadeite — especially translucency, internal life, texture and presence in hand.

This is one reason serious buyers often prefer in-person viewing before making a final decision.
