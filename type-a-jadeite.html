What is Type A Jadeite?

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated, dyed, or polymer impregnated. It keeps its original mineral structure, natural colour, and integrity.

This is the first question every serious jadeite buyer should understand. Before colour, price, rarity, or setting, treatment status gives the piece its foundation.

What does Type A mean?

Type A jadeite means the jadeite remains natural, without chemical bleaching, artificial dyeing, or polymer impregnation.

In simple terms, the stone has not been chemically altered to improve its appearance. Its colour, structure, and internal character belong to the original material.

Answer

Does Type A mean high quality?

No. Type A describes treatment status. It does not automatically mean the jadeite is beautiful, rare, translucent, or valuable.

A Type A jadeite piece still needs to be judged by colour, translucency, texture, structure, proportion, carving, setting, and overall presence.

Two Type A pieces can differ greatly in value. One may be quiet and ordinary; another may be luminous, balanced, and deeply collectible.

Type A tells you the stone has integrity. Quality tells you how special it is.

Type A is not the end of jadeite knowledge. It is where proper judgement begins.

Verification

How can Type A jadeite be checked?

Jadeite may be examined through gemological testing, including refractive index, specific gravity, spectroscopy, and microscopic observation.

A refractive index of approximately 1.66 is one recognised indicator associated with jadeite jade, but proper identification should be done by a qualified gemological laboratory.

Buyer Clarity

What should you ask before choosing jadeite?

A serious jadeite purchase should never begin with price alone. It should begin with clarity: what the stone is, whether it has been treated, and how it appears in person.

Is it jadeite?

Jadeite and nephrite are not the same mineral. Clear identification matters.

Is it Type A?

Type A jadeite has not been chemically treated, dyed, or polymer impregnated.

Does it suit me?

Colour, translucency, proportion, and presence should be viewed on the person.

Jadeite changes with light, angle, skin tone, and distance. A photograph may introduce the piece, but it cannot fully reveal its texture, depth, glow, and presence.

This is why private viewing matters. You can compare pieces slowly, ask questions, and understand what your eyes are seeing.
