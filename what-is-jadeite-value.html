What gives jadeite its value?

Colour matters, but colour alone is not enough. Fine jadeite value is shaped by nature, structure, light, rarity, workmanship and whether the stone remains natural Type A.

Quick answer: jadeite value is influenced by colour, translucency, texture, structure, thickness, craftsmanship, rarity, size, proportion and Type A status.

Two green jadeite bangles can look similar to a beginner, yet differ greatly in value because one may have finer texture, stronger translucency, better proportions, fewer internal issues and better colour distribution.

The Real Question

Why can two green jadeite bangles have completely different prices?

Jadeite is not priced like a factory-made object. It is a natural material, and each piece carries its own combination of colour, translucency, grain, structure, thickness, internal features and cutting decisions.

A beginner may see only “green”. A trained eye looks for how the green lives inside the stone: is it vivid, even, dull, patchy, floating, concentrated, deep, fresh, watery, dry, fine or coarse?

Jadeite value is shaped by colour, translucency, texture, structure, thickness, craftsmanship and rarity.

The more quietly beautiful jadeite appears under real light, the more serious the evaluation becomes. True value is rarely decided by one factor alone.

The 7 Value Factors

The main factors that affect jadeite value.

01

Colour

Strong, lively and well-distributed colour can increase jadeite value, especially vivid green, fine lavender and attractive icy tones.

02

Translucency

Fine jadeite allows light to enter and move through the stone. Better translucency often gives jadeite a more luminous, watery appearance.

03

Texture

Fine texture looks smoother, cleaner and more refined. Coarse texture may appear grainy, dry or less elegant under light.

04

Structure

Internal cracks, visible lines, uneven mineral growth or weak structure can affect both beauty and durability.

05

Thickness

For bangles and carved pieces, thickness affects presence, strength, comfort, light play and material usage.

06

Craftsmanship

Cutting, polishing, carving and setting must respect the jadeite. Poor workmanship can weaken even a beautiful stone.

07

Rarity

Fine natural jadeite with beautiful colour, good translucency, clean texture and balanced proportions is rare.

A

Type A status

Natural Type A jadeite has not been chemically bleached, dyed or polymer-impregnated. This matters greatly for serious buyers.

Fit

Suitability

The right jadeite must also suit the wearer: wrist size, lifestyle, purpose, taste, comfort and long-term meaning.

Visual Education

Translucency, colour, texture and light must be judged together.

Jadeite should be viewed under real light because cameras may exaggerate colour, hide texture or flatten translucency. A serious viewing should compare the stone in natural daylight, indoor light and against the wearer’s skin tone.

Translucency affects how light enters and moves through jadeite.

Colour must be judged by tone, freshness, distribution and harmony.

Fine texture gives jadeite a smoother and more refined presence.

Jadeite can shift under daylight, warm light, cool light and shade.

Type A Matters

Why natural Type A jadeite is important.

Type A jadeite refers to natural jadeite that has not been chemically bleached, dyed or polymer-impregnated. It may be traditionally polished, but the jadeite itself remains natural.
