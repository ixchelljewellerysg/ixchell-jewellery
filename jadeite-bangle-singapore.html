What is a jadeite bangle?

A jadeite bangle is a continuous circular form carved from a single piece of jadeite. It is worn directly on the wrist and is valued not only for its appearance, but for its balance of colour, translucency, and structure.

In short: a jadeite bangle is one of the most complete expressions of jadeite — uninterrupted, and therefore unforgiving in quality.

What types of jadeite bangles are there?

There are four main types: round (圓條), flat (正圈), oval (貴妃), and carved bangles.

Round bangles feel traditional and balanced. Flat bangles sit closer to the wrist. Oval bangles are easier to wear. Carved bangles carry artistic and cultural meaning.

The difference is not only visual — it is how the piece feels when worn.

What colours do jadeite bangles come in?

Jadeite appears in a wide spectrum: green, lavender, icy white, yellow, red, and multi-colour combinations such as 春帶彩.

Each colour carries a different presence. Green represents vitality and value. Lavender feels calm and refined. Icy white reflects clarity and purity.

In short: colour is what draws attention — but it should never be the only factor.

What size jadeite bangle should I choose?

Jadeite bangle size is determined by inner diameter, typically measured in millimetres. Comfort depends on both size and hand structure.

A proper fit should slide over the hand with slight resistance, then rest comfortably on the wrist without pressure.

In practice: sizing is best confirmed in person, as proportion matters as much as measurement.

Why are jadeite bangles important in history and culture?

Jadeite bangles have long been associated with protection, continuity, and family heritage across Chinese and Southeast Asian cultures.

They are often passed from one generation to another — not as decoration, but as something kept, worn, and remembered.

You do not truly “own” a jadeite bangle. You keep it for the next generation.

Can I wear a jadeite bangle in a hot shower or onsen?

This is one of the most common questions. Natural Type A jadeite is a stable mineral and is not affected by water or normal heat exposure such as showers or onsen.

However, not all jadeite is the same. Treated jadeite may have undergone processes that affect long-term stability under heat or moisture.

Extreme heat, sudden temperature changes, or impact should always be approached with awareness.

In short: the concern is not jadeite itself, but the integrity of the material.

How do I choose the right jadeite bangle?

Start with Type A jadeite. Then consider colour, translucency, texture, proportion, and how the piece feels when worn.

Most people begin with colour — but the better approach is balance.

The right bangle is not the most striking one. It is the one that feels naturally right on you.
