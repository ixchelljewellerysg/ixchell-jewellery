Type A vs B vs C Jadeite

The most important jadeite question is not colour. It is treatment.

Understand this once, and everything becomes clear.

What is the difference?

Type A

Natural jadeite. No chemical treatment. No dye.

Type B

Chemically treated and resin filled to improve appearance.

Type C

Dyed jadeite. Colour is altered or enhanced artificially.

Type Meaning Impact
Type A Natural jadeite Highest integrity
Type B Chemical + resin Altered structure
Type C Dyed Artificial colour

What is actually touching your skin?

Jadeite is often worn daily, sometimes for years. The more relevant question is not whether it looks beautiful, but what material remains in contact with your skin over time.

Treatment does not only affect appearance. It affects structure, stability, and the nature of the surface you are wearing.

Type A

Natural mineral structure. No polymer, no dye. What touches your skin remains consistent over time.

Stable. Calm. Reliable for daily wear.

Type B

Internally altered through chemical process and resin. Surface may feel similar, but structure is no longer original.

Looks improved. Integrity reduced.

Type C

Colour enhanced artificially. Stability of colour may change depending on environment and exposure.

Visually attractive, but not naturally formed.

Does jadeite affect the skin?

Jadeite is not a medicinal material. It does not “treat” the skin in a scientific sense.

What matters is whether the material is natural, stable, and suitable for long-term wear.

The difference is not what jadeite does to you — it is what you are actually wearing.

Why does this matter?

A strong colour does not mean a better jadeite piece.

The foundation must always be natural integrity.

Authenticity first. Beauty second.
