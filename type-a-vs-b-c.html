Type A vs B vs C Jadeite

Most people see colour first. Experienced buyers ask about treatment first. Understanding the difference between Type A, Type B and Type C jadeite helps buyers avoid confusion, unrealistic expectations and misleading comparisons.

The Core Difference

Natural versus treated jadeite

Type Meaning What buyers should know
Type A Natural jadeite with no chemical bleaching, resin filling or dyeing. Preferred by collectors and buyers seeking natural Burmese jadeite.
Type B Acid-treated and commonly resin-filled to improve appearance. May appear more translucent at first glance but is no longer considered natural Type A jadeite.
Type C Artificially dyed to enhance or alter colour. Colour does not fully represent the stone’s natural state.
Why It Matters

Why experienced buyers ask this first

Fair comparison

Colour and translucency should never be compared fairly between natural and treated material without understanding treatment status first.

Long-term confidence

Natural jadeite is generally preferred for collecting, heirloom jewellery and long-term appreciation.

Knowledge before trends

Many buyers focus on intense green colour first. Serious understanding begins with authenticity and structure.
