History & Origins of Jade

Jade did not move through history as one simple object. It travelled through geology, dynasties, trade routes, migration, family memory and modern collecting culture. This bilingual guide follows jade from ancient nephrite traditions to Burmese jadeite, Qing imperial taste, Mesoamerican jadeite and the journeys of South-East Asia.

Before Modern Geology

Jade Was Loved Before It Was Named.

Long before modern geology classified minerals, people already knew jade through touch, colour, sound and meaning.

Ancient communities did not begin with chemical formulas. They began with what they could feel: coolness in the hand, weight in the palm, toughness under the tool, and a surface that seemed to hold light differently from ordinary stone.

Today, gemmology separates “jade” into two recognised mineral groups: nephrite and jadeite. They share one cultural name, but their geological stories are different.

Nephrite shaped much of early Chinese jade culture. Jadeite entered Chinese taste later, especially through Myanmar and Yunnan trade routes, and gradually changed how colour, translucency and luxury were understood.

Jade became important before science explained it.

玉，在被科學命名以前，早已被人珍視。

在現代地質學與礦物學出現以前，人們早已透過觸感、色澤、聲音與文化意義認識玉。

古人一開始不會用化學式理解玉。他們感受到的是：握在手裡的微涼、掌心裡的重量、工具下的堅韌，以及那種與普通石頭不同的含光感。

今天的寶石學，會將「玉」主要分為兩種被認可的礦物：軟玉（Nephrite）與硬玉（Jadeite）。它們共用同一個文化名稱，卻有不同的地質故事。

中國早期的玉文化，很大一部分由軟玉形塑。翡翠，也就是硬玉，則較晚透過緬甸與雲南一帶的貿易路線進入華人審美，慢慢改變人們對顏色、通透感與奢華感的理解。

在科學解釋玉以前，玉已經先被人們放進心裡。
Ancient Jade

Nephrite — The Ancient Jade

Long before jadeite became widely admired, much of early Chinese jade culture developed around nephrite.

Archaeological discoveries show nephrite appearing thousands of years ago within ritual systems, ceremonial objects, burial traditions and symbols of status.

People did not simply admire nephrite because it looked beautiful.

They respected how difficult it was.

Nephrite belongs to the amphibole mineral family. Its internal fibrous structure gives remarkable toughness. Ancient craftsmen understood this long before geology explained it.

The stone resisted force. Tools moved slowly. Patience mattered.

Some historians describe jade not simply as decoration. But as discipline.

Ancient people did not choose jade because it was easy. They chose it because it was worth effort.

軟玉 — 更早進入歷史的玉

在翡翠被廣泛認識以前，中國很大一部分早期玉文化，其實是圍繞軟玉發展。

考古發現顯示，數千年前的儀式文化、陪葬制度、禮器系統與身份象徵之中，已經能看見軟玉的存在。

古人珍視軟玉，不只是因為外觀。

更多時候，是因為它不容易。

Craftsmanship

Before Metal Machines, People Still Carved Jade.

One surprising fact about ancient jade history is this: Some jade objects were created long before modern cutting machines existed.

Nephrite is not easy to shape. Ancient craftsmen could not simply strike pieces apart.

Instead, people slowly removed material through abrasion.

Sand became technology.

Hard minerals were crushed into fine abrasive powder. Water formed slurry. Wood, bamboo, cord systems and grinding surfaces slowly shaped stone.

Some researchers studying prehistoric jade workshops discovered evidence of rotational drilling and gradual polishing methods.

People moved slowly. Stone changed slowly. Great craftsmanship often grows slowly too.

Human skill arrived before machines.

Mountain Stone

Then Came Jadeite.

For centuries, nephrite shaped much of Chinese jade culture. Then another stone gradually entered history.

Jadeite.

Different geology. Different crystal structure. Different visual possibilities.

Unlike nephrite, jadeite belongs to the pyroxene mineral group.

Its crystalline structure allows visual characteristics collectors recognise today: greater translucency potential, broader colour variation, stronger visual contrast.

Lavender. Green. White. Black. Yellow. Red. Ice-like transparency.

Mountain Origins

Where Burmese Jadeite Began.

The jadeite admired today did not begin inside jewellery workshops.

Its story began much deeper.

Northern Myanmar sits within one of the world's most important jadeite-bearing geological regions.

Deep underground, immense tectonic pressure and complex geological conditions slowly formed jadeite-bearing rock over immense periods of time.

Scientists generally understand jadeite formation as occurring under very specific high-pressure, relatively low-temperature metamorphic environments.

Not every mountain can create jadeite.

Not every geological environment can preserve it.

Nature required extraordinary conditions.

Time did the rest.

Some stones are made quickly. Jadeite belongs to geological patience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is jade the same as jadeite?

No. Jade is a cultural and commercial term that mainly refers to two recognised minerals: nephrite and jadeite. They share the same broad name, but they are different minerals.

不是。玉是一個文化與商業上常用的名稱，主要包含軟玉與硬玉兩種被認可的礦物。它們共用「玉」這個大名稱，但本質上是不同礦物。

Is nephrite fake jade?

No. Nephrite is genuine jade. It is not jadeite, but it has a very long cultural and historical importance, especially in early Chinese jade culture.

不是。軟玉是真正的玉。它不是翡翠，但在早期中國玉文化裡有非常重要的歷史與文化地位。

Why did jadeite become so important during the Qing dynasty?

Burmese jadeite offered visual qualities that differed from older nephrite traditions, including vivid colour, stronger translucency and brighter contrast. By the late Qing dynasty, jadeite became increasingly associated with elite taste and imperial jewellery culture.

緬甸翡翠帶來了與傳統軟玉不同的視覺特色，例如鮮明色彩、較高通透感與更強的視覺層次。到了清朝晚期，翡翠逐漸與上層審美與宮廷珠寶文化產生連結。

Was Empress Dowager Cixi the person who created jadeite culture?

No. Jadeite existed and entered China through trade before Cixi. A more careful historical reading is that Empress Dowager Cixi became strongly associated with vivid green Burmese jadeite and helped elevate its prestige within late Qing imperial taste.

不是。翡翠在慈禧太后以前，已經透過貿易逐漸進入中國。比較準確的說法是，慈禧太后與鮮綠色緬甸翡翠產生密切連結，也讓翡翠在晚清宮廷審美中的地位更加提升。

Did jade history only happen in Asia?

No. Ancient Mesoamerican civilisations, especially the Maya, also valued jadeite deeply. Guatemala remains an important part of the wider story of jadeite outside Asia.

不是。古代中美洲文明，尤其馬雅文明，同樣非常重視硬玉。瓜地馬拉在亞洲以外的翡翠歷史裡，也是很重要的一部分。

What is the Maritime Jade Road?

The Maritime Jade Road refers to ancient seafaring exchange networks that moved nephrite ornaments and related technologies across Taiwan, the Philippines and island Southeast Asia long before the Silk Road became famous.

海上玉路指的是古代海洋交流網絡。它讓軟玉飾品、材料與相關工藝，在台灣、菲律賓與東南亞群島之間移動，時間比絲路聞名更早。
