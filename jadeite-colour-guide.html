Jadeite Colour Guide

What Jadeite Colour
Should I Choose?

Jadeite colour is not only about appearance. It is about balance, personality, lifestyle, mood, skin tone, and how the piece feels when worn.

Green Jadeite

Classic, powerful, timeless.

Green jadeite is often associated with vitality, prosperity, confidence, and classic jadeite beauty.

Collectors who appreciate heritage elegance, traditional jadeite aesthetics, and stronger visual presence are often naturally drawn towards green jadeite.

Green jadeite usually feels confident rather than quiet.

Lavender Jadeite

Soft, elegant, emotionally calming.

Lavender jadeite feels gentle, refined, and quietly feminine.

It is especially loved by modern collectors who prefer understated elegance rather than loud visual intensity.

Lavender jadeite often feels emotional before it feels luxurious.

The best jadeite colour is not always the most expensive one. It is the colour that feels naturally yours.

Icy White Jadeite

Quiet luxury in its purest form.

Icy white jadeite suggests clarity, restraint, calmness, and sophistication.

It blends beautifully with modern wardrobes and often appeals to professionals, minimalists, and contemporary luxury collectors.

Icy white jadeite whispers rather than shouts.

Spring Colour Jadeite

Poetic balance between green and lavender.

春帶彩 jadeite combines vitality and softness within a single piece.

Each piece is highly individual and often attracts collectors who enjoy artistic beauty, personality, and emotional uniqueness.

No two spring-colour jadeite pieces ever feel exactly the same.

Dark Jadeite

Grounded, modern, powerful.

Dark jadeite and 墨翠-style tones often feel contemporary, intelligent, and quietly commanding.

They pair beautifully with monochrome fashion, watches, darker wardrobes, and sharper silhouettes.

Dark jadeite often attracts people who prefer confidence without noise.

Private Viewing

View jadeite colours properly,
not through filters.

Jadeite changes under different lighting conditions. The best way to understand colour is to compare pieces calmly in person.
