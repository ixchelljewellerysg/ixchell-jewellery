Before choosing jadeite, it helps to understand what your eyes are seeing.

Question

What is Type A jadeite?

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated, dyed, or polymer impregnated. It keeps its original mineral structure, natural colour, and integrity.

This matters because jadeite is not judged by beauty alone. Treatment, structure, texture, translucency, and verification all affect long-term value and collectability.

A confident purchase begins with proper knowledge, not pressure.

Jade is a broad word. It may refer to jadeite or nephrite. Jadeite is rarer, often more translucent, and traditionally more valued in fine jewellery.

That is why the phrase “real jade” can be confusing. The better question is: what kind of jade is it, has it been treated, and can it be verified?

At Ixchell Jewellery, we prefer clients to ask simple questions. Jadeite should feel fascinating, not intimidating.

Question

Is all jade the same?

Answer

Why Burmese jadeite?

Myanmar remains the most recognised source of gem-quality jadeite, especially for stones admired for rich colour, fine texture, translucency, and enduring strength.

Provenance alone does not make a piece beautiful. A jadeite piece must still be judged by its individual quality — its hue, tone, saturation, texture, translucency, carving, setting, and how it sits with the wearer.

The stone should not only look impressive. It should feel right.

Jadeite rewards the person who looks twice.

Verification

How can jadeite be checked?

Jadeite may be identified through gemological testing, including refractive index, specific gravity, spectroscopy, and microscopic observation.

A refractive index of approximately 1.66 is one recognised indicator associated with jadeite jade, but proper testing should always be done by a qualified gemological laboratory.

Clients are welcome to verify their pieces independently. Knowledge should make the purchase calmer, not more complicated.

Why In-Person Viewing Matters

Jadeite changes with light, skin tone, and distance.

A photograph may show colour, but it cannot fully show presence. Jadeite can appear softer, deeper, brighter, or quieter depending on light, angle, background, and the person wearing it.

This is why we encourage private viewing. You are given time to compare pieces properly, ask questions, and observe the stone without rushing.
