Understanding Jadeite, Properly

Fine jadeite should never be rushed. A little knowledge allows the eye, the hand, and the heart to recognise what is truly worth keeping.

What is Type A jadeite?

Type A jadeite refers to natural jadeite that has not undergone chemical treatment or dyeing. It retains its original structure, colour, and integrity.

Is all jade the same?

No. Jade is a general term. It may refer to jadeite or nephrite. Jadeite is rarer, more translucent, and traditionally more valued in fine jewellery.

Why is Burmese jadeite preferred?

Myanmar remains the primary source of gem-quality jadeite, known for its depth of colour, fine texture, and enduring strength.

How can jadeite be verified?

Jadeite may be identified through gemological testing, including its refractive index of approximately 1.66, a recognised indicator of jadeite jade.
