Feng Shui, observation and historical understanding

Many modern people misunderstand Feng Shui as purely religious or superstitious practice. Historically, however, Feng Shui may also be understood as an ancient system of environmental observation, spatial analysis, orientation study and probability-based interpretation developed across centuries within Chinese civilisation.

Ancient civilisations around the world developed remarkable systems of architecture, astronomy, navigation, calendar calculation, earthquake detection and environmental adaptation long before modern scientific terminology formally explained many natural phenomena.

The Alhambra in Spain, for example, still demonstrates how older civilisations understood water, airflow, cooling, geometry, proportion and emotional spatial experience with extraordinary refinement.

For this reason, some people today choose not to dismiss traditional systems immediately, but instead study them through the lens of accumulated observation, environmental understanding, human behaviour and cultural continuity.

Symbolism & Human Meaning

Symbols may not change reality directly, but meaning can influence how people experience calmness, confidence and emotional grounding.

Throughout history, humans across different cultures have attached symbolic meaning to architecture, heirlooms, talismans, jewellery and objects of continuity.

Traditional Symbolism

Symbolic jadeite carvings and their traditional meanings.

Different jadeite carvings may carry different forms of symbolism depending on cultural interpretation, family tradition and personal belief.

Bamboo

Growth, resilience and steady progress

Bamboo is traditionally associated with resilience, flexibility, integrity and continuous growth. In many Asian cultures, bamboo also carries scholarly symbolism and may be associated with stable career progression and personal discipline.

Pixiu

Wealth symbolism and business protection

Pixiu is traditionally associated with wealth symbolism, business fortune and the guarding of prosperity within Chinese cultural belief. Many business owners and entrepreneurs appreciate Pixiu through the lens of motivation, symbolism and emotional reassurance.

Kirin · Qilin

Wisdom, peace and noble protection

Kirin, also known as Qilin, is traditionally associated with wisdom, calm leadership, protection and peaceful prosperity. Historically, the creature often symbolised benevolent leadership and auspicious presence.

Longgui

Stability, endurance and longevity

Longgui symbolism is often associated with grounding, endurance, long-term stability and generational continuity. Some people appreciate Longgui symbolism for career resilience and life balance rather than short-term wealth pursuit alone.

Cultural Practices & Belief

Blessings, Kāiguāng and Zǒulú

開光 (Kāiguāng) is often translated as “blessing” or “consecration”. It refers to a traditional cultural or spiritual practice where an object is symbolically blessed through prayer, ritual or ceremonial intention.

走爐 (Zǒulú) may be understood as “passing through incense” or “passing by the incense burner”. Some jadeite owners bring their pieces near temple incense or ceremonial spaces as part of personal belief, blessing tradition or spiritual comfort.

Across many generations in Southeast Asia, jadeite has often been associated with reassurance, grounding, protection symbolism and family continuity. Some people approach these practices spiritually, while others appreciate them culturally or emotionally.
