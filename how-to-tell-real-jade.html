Real Jade Guide

How can you tell if jade is real?

Real jadeite is not identified by one “magic test”. The best approach is understanding texture, translucency, structure, treatment status and how jadeite behaves under real light.

Quick answer: real Type A jadeite should feel structurally natural, visually balanced and free from obvious dye concentration, plastic-like appearance or unnatural surface behaviour.

The safest approach is understanding whether the jadeite is Type A, Type B or Type C.

First Understanding

“Real jade” is more complicated than people think.

Many people search online for “how to tell if jade is real” hoping for a quick scratch test, flashlight trick or sound test.

Unfortunately, jadeite is more complicated than that.

Some fake materials can imitate colour surprisingly well in photographs. At the same time, some natural jadeite may look less impressive online than it does in person.

Important Checks

What should you actually look for?

Type A Status

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite without chemical bleaching, dyeing or polymer impregnation.

Natural Texture

Real jadeite usually shows natural crystal structure rather than a perfectly flat plastic appearance.

Light Behaviour

Natural jadeite reacts differently under changing light and often has depth rather than surface-only colour.

Important: many “home jade tests” online are unreliable. Sound tests, scratch tests and temperature tests alone cannot confirm Type A jadeite properly.

Real jadeite reveals itself slowly, not loudly.

Type A vs B vs C

Understanding Type A, B and C jadeite.

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated or dyed.

Type B jadeite has usually been chemically bleached and polymer impregnated to improve appearance.

Type C jadeite has been artificially dyed to change colour intensity.

Some jadeite may also contain combinations of treatments.

This is why proper explanation and trustworthy sourcing matter when buying jadeite jewellery.

Real Life Viewing

Why viewing jadeite in person matters.

Lighting changes everything.

Camera filters, overexposure, editing and strong jewellery lighting can dramatically change how jadeite appears online.

This is why many experienced collectors prefer to compare jadeite under daylight, indoor light and natural movement before deciding.

FAQ

Common questions about real jade.

How can I tell if jade is real?

Real jadeite should be judged through structure, translucency, texture, treatment status and professional gemmological evaluation.

Can fake jade look real?

Yes. Glass, serpentine, quartz and treated jadeite can sometimes resemble real jadeite in photographs or poor lighting.

What is Type A jadeite?

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite without chemical bleaching, dyeing or polymer impregnation.

Can I test jade at home?

Home tests are limited and not fully reliable for serious identification. Proper gemmological testing remains the safest approach.
