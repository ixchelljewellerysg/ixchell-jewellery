Buyer Clarity

Do not buy jade by colour alone.

Many people search for “where to buy jade” and begin by asking for green colour or price. That is understandable, but jadeite buying needs more care.

The first question is not “how green is it?” The better question is: is it jadeite or nephrite, is it natural Type A jadeite, and how does it behave under real light?

Jadeite can look very different under daylight, warm light, cool light, shade and camera exposure. This is why in-person viewing remains important.

Before Buying

What should you check before buying jade?

1. Jade or Jadeite?

Jade is a broad word. Jadeite and nephrite are different minerals. If you are buying fine jade jewellery, this distinction matters.

2. Type A Status

Type A jadeite is natural jadeite that has not been chemically treated, dyed or polymer impregnated.

3. Real Viewing

View jadeite under real light. Photos can exaggerate colour, translucency and texture.

Simple rule: a good jadeite seller should be able to explain the piece calmly. If everything is only “very green, very lucky, very cheap”, pause first. Quiet luxury prefers clarity.

The right place to buy jade is the place that helps you understand before you choose.

FAQ

Common questions about buying jade in Singapore.

Where can I buy jade in Singapore?

You can buy jade from jadeite specialists, jewellery boutiques and private viewing studios. For serious jadeite buying, choose a seller who explains jadeite vs nephrite, Type A status, treatment, translucency, texture and certification clearly.

What should I look for when buying jade?

Look for correct identification, Type A status, colour, translucency, texture, thickness, proportion, workmanship and how the piece appears under real light.

Is Type A jadeite important?

Yes. Type A jadeite is natural jadeite without chemical treatment, dyeing or polymer impregnation. It is usually the preferred starting point for serious jadeite buyers.

Can I test jade at home?

Home tests are not reliable enough for serious buying. Jadeite identification is best supported by gemmological testing and proper professional explanation.
