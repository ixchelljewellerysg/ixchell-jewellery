Answer First

How do you choose a jade bangle?

To choose a jade bangle, first check the fit, then observe the structure, translucency, colour distribution and how it looks under different lighting.

Natural Type A jadeite may appear different under daylight, indoor light and shadow. This is why viewing in person is especially important.

Not Colour Alone

The first mistake is judging only by colour.

Colour attracts the eye, but structure tells the deeper story. A bangle may look bright in a photograph yet feel different in person.

The better question is not “which is cheaper?” but “which one has the presence, structure and feeling that I would still choose years later?”

Value Without Price War

Jadeite value is not a race to the lowest price.

Ixchell Jewellery does not position jadeite by price comparison. We help clients understand what they are seeing before they decide.

Three Things To Observe

When viewing a bangle, slow down here.

1. Fit

The bangle should pass the hand properly without becoming uncomfortable or unsafe to wear.

2. Structure

Look at the internal texture, grain and natural formation. This is where many differences begin.

3. Light

View it under different angles. Jadeite changes visually as light enters and leaves the stone.

Carved Bangles

Carving is not automatically a flaw cover.

A carved jadeite bangle can be chosen for artistry, symbolism and craftsmanship. It should not be dismissed simply because it is carved.

The fair way to understand a carved bangle is to examine the whole piece — design, workmanship, structure and natural character.

A serious bangle is not chosen in one glance.

It is turned in the hand. Seen in light. Compared quietly with your own instinct.

FAQ

Jade Bangle Questions

Can jade change colour over time?

Natural jadeite does not truly change into a different material. It may look slightly different due to lighting, surface condition, body oil, cleaning habits or photography.

Does a jade bangle become greener when worn?

A jade bangle does not magically become green. It may appear more lively when the surface is clean, polished and viewed in better light.

Is a carved jade bangle bad?

No. Carving itself does not make a bangle bad. It should be judged by naturalness, workmanship, structure, proportion and overall beauty.
