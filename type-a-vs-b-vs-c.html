Type A vs Type B vs Type C Jadeite

The difference most people never learn — and why it changes everything.

The truth most shops will not explain clearly

When people hear “jade”, they often assume all jade is the same. It is not.

In reality, jadeite is divided into three main categories: Type A, Type B and Type C.

The difference is not small. It defines authenticity, durability, and long-term value.

Type A is natural. Type B and C are treated.

What is Type A jadeite?

Type A jadeite is completely natural jadeite.

It has not been chemically treated, dyed, or artificially enhanced.

What you see is what formed naturally over millions of years.

This is why Type A jadeite holds the highest value and is the only type accepted by serious collectors.

What is Type B jadeite?

Type B jadeite has been chemically treated.

Acid is used to remove impurities inside the stone, making it appear clearer.

After that, resin is injected to stabilise the structure.

While it may look attractive at first, the internal structure has been altered.

Over time, Type B jadeite can lose clarity, become dull, or even degrade.

What is Type C jadeite?

Type C jadeite has been artificially dyed.

Colour is added to enhance or imitate higher quality jadeite.

This colour is not natural and can fade over time.

What appears vibrant today may not remain the same in the future.

Why this matters more than most realise

At a glance, Type A, B and C can look similar — especially in photographs.

But their nature is completely different.

Type A — natural, stable, holds value

Type B — chemically altered, structurally changed

Type C — dyed, colour not natural

This is why experienced buyers do not choose jadeite quickly.

They observe, compare, and understand before deciding.

Why Burmese Type A jadeite stands apart

While jadeite can be found in different parts of the world, high-quality gem-grade jadeite primarily comes from Myanmar (Burma).

This is where the finest structure, translucency and colour are formed naturally.

Not all jadeite is equal — origin matters.

The real difference is not price — it is understanding

Many first-time buyers focus on appearance alone.

But jadeite is not something that should be judged only by colour or brightness.

The true difference lies in what the stone actually is.

Once you understand this, your way of choosing jadeite changes completely.
