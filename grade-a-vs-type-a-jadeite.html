Why “Type A Jadeite” Is the Only Correct Term — And Why “Grade A Jade” Is Misleading

In today’s jade market, one of the most misunderstood expressions is “Grade A jade”. It sounds familiar, reassuring, and easy to understand — but from a gemological perspective, it is not the proper term.

The correct term is Type A jadeite. And the difference is not merely semantic. It is the difference between technical accuracy and market shorthand, between scientific clarity and casual assumption.

Example of an NGI gemological report clearly stating “Natural Fei Cui (Jadeite Jade, Type A)” — not “Grade A jade”.

The difference between “grade” and “type”

In gemology, the word grade is generally used to describe quality characteristics such as colour, clarity or cut. This is familiar in diamonds and many other gemstones.
