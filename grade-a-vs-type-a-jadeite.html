Grade A Jade vs Type A Jadeite

Most people search for Grade A jade. Very few realise that this is not the correct term.

The confusion is common. In jadeite, however, clarity matters.

Direct answer: “Grade A jade” is not a recognised gemological term. The correct and internationally accepted term is Type A jadeite.

Why the confusion exists

The term Grade A jade is often used in online listings, marketplaces, and casual conversations. It sounds reassuring, but it is not used in proper gemological classification.

In reality, jadeite is classified based on treatment, not “grade”. This is where the misunderstanding begins.

What Type A jadeite actually means

Type A jadeite refers to jadeite that is completely natural and has not been chemically treated.

  • Not chemically treated
  • Not dyed
  • Not resin-filled

This is the only form of jadeite recognised as natural in gemological reports.

What “Grade A jade” does not tell you

When a piece is described as Grade A jade, it does not confirm whether it is natural jadeite, treated jadeite, or even another material entirely.

For buyers looking for real jadeite in Singapore, this lack of precision creates unnecessary uncertainty.

Why this matters when buying jadeite

Jadeite is not judged by colour alone. Its character depends on structure, translucency, natural colour formation, and whether it remains untreated.

A piece may appear attractive in photographs, yet differ significantly in person. This is why many collectors prefer to view jadeite directly.

Real jadeite in Singapore

In Singapore, natural jadeite is properly referred to as Type A jadeite, especially in certified pieces.

At Ixchell Jewellery, the focus remains on natural Type A Burmese jadeite, accurate terminology, and a quieter, more considered way of choosing.

How to verify jadeite properly

The most reliable way to confirm jadeite authenticity is through a recognised gemological report.

In Singapore, this is commonly issued by Nanyang Gemological Institute (NGI). Clients may also choose to verify their jadeite with any trusted laboratory independently.

Confidence in jadeite is not based on wording, but on what can be properly examined and confirmed.
